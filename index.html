Wed, Jun 24 · Crater Lake deep-dive

Hikes for the Crater Lake day.

Heads up: Cleetwood Cove Trail — the only legal route down to the water — is closed through 2026 for a multi-year rim and dock restoration. That's the swim-in-Crater-Lake hike off the table for this trip. The next-best-thing list below stays on the rim and gets us the giant blue views without a big day. All under 1,300 ft of climb, all 5 miles or less.

Top pick

Garfield Peak Trail

3.6 miout & back 1,010 ftvertical Moderate2–3 hrs

The classic intermediate Crater Lake hike. Starts right behind Crater Lake Lodge on the south rim, climbs steadily through wildflowers and stunted hemlocks, and tops out at 8,054 ft with a full-frame view of the lake, Wizard Island, and Mt. Scott across the caldera. This is the one to do if you only do one.

Trailhead: Crater Lake Lodge. Snow can linger on the upper switchbacks into early July — check ranger station the morning of.

Read more on AllTrails →
Bigger views, bigger climb

Mt. Scott Trail

4.4 miout & back 1,250 ftvertical Moderate+3–4 hrs

Highest point in the park (8,929 ft) and a working fire lookout at the summit. 360° views: Crater Lake one direction, the high desert and Cascades the other. Trailhead is on the east side of Rim Drive — pair it with the full loop drive.

Trailhead: east side of Rim Drive, ~14 miles from Rim Village. Exposed; bring sun protection.

Read more on AllTrails →
Short & scenic

Watchman Peak Trail

1.6 miout & back 420 ftvertical Easy+45–75 min

The shortest route to a real summit view. Old fire lookout on top, best sunset spot in the park, and easy enough that the whole group can do it after dinner at the lodge. Good fallback if Garfield's upper switchbacks are still snowed in.

Trailhead: Watchman Overlook parking, west rim.

Read more on AllTrails →
If you want a flat-ish walk

Discovery Point Trail

2.2 miout & back 200 ftvertical Easy1 hr

A flat rim walk from Rim Village to Discovery Point (where Crater Lake was "discovered" by settlers in 1853). Lake views the whole way, no real climb, dog-friendly. Perfect if any of the group wants to skip the bigger hikes and still get on a trail.

Trailhead: Rim Village, near the visitor center.

Fri, Jun 26 · Portland finale

Where we'll eat in Portland.

Six picks for a memorable closing-night dinner for six — fun rooms, chef-driven food, and a touch upscale without going stuffy. Book early; Portland's best rooms fill 30–60 days out.

The pick

Kann

SE Portland · Haitian, wood-fired · $$$

Gregory Gourdet's wood-fired Haitian restaurant. James Beard Best New Restaurant (2023) and Best Chef Northwest winner. Live-fire cooking, Caribbean flavor, and one of the most energetic dining rooms in Portland. The restaurant the whole table will keep talking about.

548 SE Ash St · Book ~60 days out via Resy. Ask for the bar or a high-top if the dining room is full.

Open Kann's site →

Le Pigeon

East Burnside · French, neo-bistro · $$$$

Gabe Rucker's tiny, iconic counter-and-tables restaurant. James Beard Best Chef Northwest. Foie gras profiteroles and pigeon as actual menu items. Get the five- or seven-course chef's tasting menu if you want the full experience.

738 E Burnside · Reserve a 6-top early — the room is small. Tasting menu pre-paid via Tock.

Open Le Pigeon's site →

República

Pearl District · Modern Mexican tasting · $$$$

Modern Mexican tasting menu from the Republica & Co. team. Multi-course, ingredient-driven, mole-forward, and quietly one of the most ambitious rooms in the city. Beautiful for a celebratory family dinner.

315 NW 13th Ave · Single seating; reservations on Tock.

Open República's site →

Langbaan

SE Division · Royal Thai tasting · $$$$

A hidden 24-seat tasting-menu room tucked behind PaaDee. Royal Thai cuisine, ~10 courses, seasonal menus that rotate. James Beard Best Chef Northwest. Hardest reservation on this list — go for this one first.

6 SE 28th Ave · Reservations drop on Tock 60 days in advance, 10am PT. Set a reminder.

Open Langbaan's site →

Coquine

Mt. Tabor · Seasonal NW bistro · $$$

Neighborhood-meets-fine-dining on the slope of Mt. Tabor. Wood grill, Pacific NW ingredients, an unfussy room, and the famous salted chocolate chip cookies. Easier 6-top than Le Pigeon, still very much a memorable meal.

6839 SE Belmont St · Reservations on Resy. Sit by the open kitchen if you can.

Open Coquine's site →

OK Omens

SE Belmont · Wine bar from Castagna · $$$

The wine bar from the Castagna team — natural wine list, smart small plates, and a much more relaxed vibe than the tasting-menu options. Best fallback / pre-dinner spot if reservations elsewhere fall through.

1758 SE Hawthorne Blvd · Walk-in friendly for a 6-top early in the evening; reservations on Resy.

Open OK Omens' site →
